## Notes

- Aschaffenburg (~71k inhabitants, ~50k eligible voters) operates ~50 Urnen-Stimmbezirke for federal elections. 2025 result pages confirm Stimmbezirk numbering up to at least 0048; numbering with 11x extends into Briefwahl bezirke. Tabular WBZ-level data is fully published; only polygon geometries are missing.
- The street-to-Stimmbezirk crosswalk (`opendata-strassen.csv` per OK.VOTE convention) is published with each election bundle. Combined with cadastral street geometries (LDBV / basemap.de), this is the latent input from which WBZ polygons are derived in ABGIS — i.e. polygons exist internally, just unpublished.
- ABGIS uses Masterportal (open source); a number of Masterportal deployments expose `/services-internet.json` and WFS endpoints. None were documented in the public Impressum; would need to ask abgis@aschaffenburg.de for the service URL or a one-time Shapefile/GeoJSON export.
- Bydata Aschaffenburg portal article (digital.aschaffenburg.de, 10/2024) explicitly names "Verkehr, Umwelt, Soziodemografie und Infrastruktur" as planned future categories — Wahlbezirke not on the roadmap; an explicit request would be needed to add them.
- Govdata.de search returns no Aschaffenburg-sourced datasets at all (city is not yet a feeding portal).
- Stimmbezirksverzeichnis PDF (https://www.aschaffenburg.de/loadDocument.phtml?FID=4173.1779.1&Ext=PDF) gives the authoritative street-list-per-Stimmbezirk for the current cycle — useful as fallback if polygons cannot be obtained.
- Recommended outreach order: (1) abgis@aschaffenburg.de cc daniel.altemeyer-bartscher@aschaffenburg.de — request Stimmbezirk polygons (Shape/GeoJSON) for BTW 2013/2017/2021/2025, asking specifically whether ABGIS Masterportal exposes a WFS for the layer; (2) wahlamt@aschaffenburg.de — holds the authoritative Wahlbezirkseinteilung per cycle; can confirm whether historical 2013/2017 cuts still exist or only the current.

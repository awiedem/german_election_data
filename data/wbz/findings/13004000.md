## Notes

- Schwerin has no open-data portal (no opendata.schwerin.de). The "Geoshop der Landeshauptstadt Schwerin" is operated by Kreis-LUP Geodatenmanagement; its catalogue is cadastre/orthophoto/Bauleitplanung — no Wahlbezirke layer.
- GeoPortal-MV / GeoMIS-MV / GovData / GDI-DE GeoNetwork return zero hits for Schwerin Wahlbezirke; M-V is absent from the Wahlbezirke keyword index entirely (NRW, NI, BB, HB, SH, HH, SN have entries — MV does not).
- Schwerin's `Raumgliederung` WMS (https://geoportal.kreis-lup.de/ows/masterportal/raumgliederung-sn) publishes Stadtgrenze/Stadtbezirke/Stadtteile/Baublöcke/PLZ — confirmed NOT WBZ. Stadtteile (~17 boroughs / 26 quarters) ≠ the ~63 Urnenwahlbezirke.
- BTW WBZ count: 63 Urnenwahlbezirke for BTW 2021 and BTW 2025 (same layout). BTW 2013 and BTW 2017 counts not stated in the sources reviewed; likely 60–65 based on continuity, but should be confirmed via Wahlbehörde when requesting historical files. Restructuring to 55 WBZ took effect after BTW 2025 (Sept 2025+), so does not affect the four target elections.
- Recommended outreach: (1) wahlbehoerde@schwerin.de — primary, owns the WBZ definitions and likely holds shape/CAD source files internally (city map shop is operated for them by Kreis-LUP); explicitly request shp/GeoJSON for BTW 2013, 2017, 2021, 2025 boundaries. (2) cc geodatenmanagement@kreis-lup.de — operates Schwerin's Masterportal/Geoshop and could publish or extract from internal layers. (3) LAiV only as last fallback; they do not maintain municipal WBZ.
- The 2021 brochure PDF is image-only (DCTDecode JPEGs of map tiles), not georeferenced. It can document boundaries visually for digitization but is not a substitute for vector data.
- Honest assessment: probability of obtaining usable geometries via email is moderate. Schwerin's Wahlbehörde does not appear to publish geo formats but almost certainly maintains WBZ as polygons in the city GIS for districting (the 2025 restructuring required it). A direct request naming all four BTW years and citing the 2025 restructuring is the right move.

## Notes

- Stadt Straubing (~47k inhabitants, kreisfreie Stadt) has NO municipal open-data portal. The only "OpenData" offering is the Bayern KM6 election-software export on `wahl.straubing.de` — purely tabular CSVs (results per Stimmbezirk + a `opendata-strassen.csv` street-to-WBZ assignment). This is the standard output for ~all Bavarian municipalities and never includes polygon geometries.
- The state-level ADBV Straubing (Amt für Digitalisierung, Breitband und Vermessung, poststelle@va-sr.bayern.de, Wittelsbacherhöhe 3) handles Liegenschaftskataster for the region — NOT relevant for municipal WBZ; do not contact.
- Recommended outreach order: (1) `vermessung@straubing.de` cc `wahlamt@straubing.de` — this is the city Geoinformation team that maintains spatial layers and would draw the WBZ polygons jointly with the Wahlamt; ask for SHP/GeoJSON of BTW 2013/2017/2021/2025 in-person Wahlbezirke under DL-DE→Zero or CC-BY. (2) If silent, follow up with `wahlamt@straubing.de` directly (they hold the authoritative Wahlbezirkseinteilung). (3) FragDenStaat IFG fallback (Bayerisches IFG covers municipal data only partially but precedent exists for boundary requests).
- Expected feature count ~30 (in-person) per BTW 2025 Stimmbezirk URL evidence (highest ID seen: 029). Briefwahlbezirke are typically separate non-spatial polling areas.
- The street-to-WBZ table (`opendata-strassen.csv`) is a useful fallback: combined with an OSM street layer it would allow approximate reconstruction of WBZ polygons via Voronoi/street-buffer methods if the city declines to share geometries.

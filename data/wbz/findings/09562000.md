## Notes

- Erlangen's Statistikamt is the data steward. The portal at https://erlangen.de/aktuelles/opendata lists Geometriedaten -> Wahlbezirke for vintages 2008, 2013, 2017, 2019, plus separate Europawahlbezirke 2014, 2017, 2019, 2025.
- "Wahlbezirke" here = allgemeine Urnen-Wahlbezirke (in-person polling districts). They are NOT the 9 Statistische Bezirke (city is divided into 9) or the ~40 Stadtteile. Polygon counts of 98-103 confirm WBZ granularity. (Target ~70 in the prompt is in the right order of magnitude; Erlangen runs ~100 WBZ for BTW.)
- 2025 anomaly: only "Europawahlbezirke 2025" (62 polygons) is published; no "Wahlbezirke 2025" entry. Search hits and the Feb 2025 file date both indicate this geometry was used for the BTW on 2025-02-23. The drop from 103 -> 62 between 2019 and 2025 reflects pandemic-era and post-2021 consolidation of small bezirke; verify against per-WBZ result counts before harmonizing.
- All four files are open under dl-by-de/2.0, free reuse with attribution.
- CRS varies across vintages (WGS84 for 2013, GK zone 4 for 2017, UTM 32N for 2019 and 2025) — must reproject to a common CRS before any spatial join.
- DBF schema is consistent across 2013-2019: XTRID, BEZIRKSTYP, NAME, NUMMER, x, y (centroid). The 2025 EW file likely uses similar fields (not extracted here).
- Published BTW 2025 results PDF (https://erlangen.de/uwao-api/faila/files/bypath/Dokumente/Statistik/Statistik%20Aktuell/13-4_b_2025_2.pdf) was retrieved but its text layer is not extractable; for an exact 2025 WBZ count, query statistik@stadt.erlangen.de directly.
- govdata.de and open.bydata.de mirror the same files via the Erlangen feed; URLs above are the canonical primary source.

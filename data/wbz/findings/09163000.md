## Notes

- Stadt Rosenheim does NOT operate an open-data portal. Its only public geodata page (`rosenheim.de/online-dienste/digitale-karten/`) embeds Google-Maps locations and lists no downloadable layers.
- The Bavarian federated portal `open.bydata.de` returns no WBZ dataset for Rosenheim (only general "Bevoelkerung" tables under AGS 09163). Munich is the only Bavarian city publishing WBZ polygons through bydata, confirming Rosenheim has not contributed any.
- The Kommunalwahl 2026 page publishes `Stadtplan_48_Stimmbezirke.pdf` and `Stimmbezirke_Kommunalwahl_2026.pdf` — a static PDF map of the 48 Kommunalwahl-Stimmbezirke and a polling-location directory. PDF only, no vector.
- BTW 2025 used 30 in-person Stimmbezirke (numbered 1-30) plus 30 Briefwahlbezirke (71-100); historical BTW counts (2013/2017/2021) are likely similar (~30-35) but only the per-bezirk result HTML pages are exposed, not the polygons.
- The municipal Stadtplanungsamt - Geoinformation und Vermessung handles all internal city surveying ("alle vermessungstechnischen Arbeiten ... fuer die Stadtverwaltung Rosenheim"). It explicitly does NOT serve private surveying requests (those go to the state ADBV). For municipal election geometry the city office is the right primary contact.
- ADBV Rosenheim (poststelle@adbv-ro.bayern.de, Muenchener Str. 23) is the state surveying office (Liegenschaftskataster, ALKIS). It is NOT relevant for WBZ polygons — these are maintained by the city Wahlamt / Stadtplanungsamt, not the state.
- Recommended outreach: (1) stadtplanung@rosenheim.de cc wahlamt@rosenheim.de requesting WBZ shapefiles for BTW 2013/2017/2021/2025 (and ideally for the 48 Kommunalwahl-Stimmbezirke as the underlying base layer); (2) phone 08031 365-1641 (Stadtplanung) or 08031 365-1364 (Wahlamt) if email lapses. The PDF Stadtplan implies a CAD/GIS source layer exists internally.

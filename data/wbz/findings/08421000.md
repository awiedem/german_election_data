## Notes

- Ulm has reputational open-data leadership (daten.ulm.de runs since ~2013 with shp/gml/geojson/csv exports), but the public catalog does NOT expose Wahlbezirke. Searches across daten.ulm.de, daten-bw.de, govdata.de, and Google site-scoped queries returned zero WBZ hits for Ulm.
- daten.ulm.de portal URL serves only a 353-byte JSON SPA shell to non-browser clients; direct catalog scraping was not possible. Search engine cache shows the catalog has thematic categories (mobility, environment, OSM) — Wahlen/Wahlbezirke is not a published category.
- Wahllokalfinder app exists at https://portal.ulm.de/mapapps/resources/apps/wahllokalfinder/ (Esri/mapapps stack). The underlying FeatureService is not exposed on the public landing page (config.json returned 404 at guessed paths); fetching the actual app HTML yields a JS bootstrapper. The polling-place geometry layer is presumably served from an internal ArcGIS instance — the Vermessungsamt would be the right contact to request export.
- BTW 2025 Ulm: 90 Urnen-WBZ + 59 Briefwahlbezirke = 149 total (per Stadt Ulm press release / search snippets). Confirms target ~70–90 polygons, well above the 18 Stadtteile.
- BW state-level FOIA precedent (FragDenStaat, request 4): Statistisches Landesamt and Landeswahlleitung confirmed they hold no WBZ geodata for any BW municipality — explicitly directs requesters to municipalities. Confirms Ulm holds the data but has not published it.
- Recommended outreach order: (1) vermessung@ulm.de (operates daten.ulm.de geometry side, holds WBZ polygons), CC (2) statistik@ulm.de (Wahlamt sets WBZ boundaries), with explicit ask for BTW 2013/2017/2021/2025 polygons in shp/gpkg/geojson. Frame as a daten.ulm.de publication candidate — Ulm's open-data culture makes a positive response likely.
- Coordinates noted in earlier search: daten.ulm.de offers downloads in GK3, GK4, ETRS89, WGS84, Google Mercator — request ETRS89 / UTM 32N (EPSG:25832) for BW consistency.

## Notes

- Dortmund uses Opendatasoft platform; for any dataset, append `/api/explore/v2.1/catalog/datasets/<id>/exports/{geojson|shp|csv|kml}` to download. This works for `stimmbezirke-2025` (polygons confirmed) and likely for historical boundary files.
- Boundary vs. results: BTW-result datasets (`fb33-bundestagswahl-YYYYMMDD-...-wahlbezirke`) appear to carry tabular results; verified 2021 sample returned `geometry:null`. Polygons live in dedicated boundary datasets like `stimmbezirke-2025`. For 2013/2017/2021 the analogous `stimmbezirke-2013/2017/2020` boundary datasets may exist but were not surfaced in search — query via API: `https://open-data.dortmund.de/api/explore/v2.1/catalog/datasets?q=stimmbezirke`.
- 2017-2020 boundaries: portal note states "302 Stimmbezirke" applied 2009-2014 and "386 Stimmbezirke" from 2020 Kommunalwahl onward. BTW 2013 uses 302; BTW 2017 likely uses 302 still or transitional; BTW 2021/2025 use 386+.
- Interactive viewer: https://geoweb1.digistadtdo.de/OWSServiceProxy/client/wahlbezirke.jsp (WMS-based, not direct shapefile).
- Open geodata catalog at https://geoweb1.digistadtdo.de/doris_gdi/opengeodata/statistik/ may host historical Stadtbezirke/Statistische Bezirke shapes; election-bezirke not listed there explicitly.
- Recommend emailing katasteramt@dortmund.de + opendata@dortmund.de jointly for historical Stimmbezirke shapefiles (2013, 2017 boundaries) since only 2024/2025 boundary datasets are surfaced on the portal.
- License DL-DE Zero 2.0 confirmed via https://opendata.dortmund.de/Informationsportal/lizenz.html.

## Notes

- Live WFS is the right artefact: layer `gebietseinheiten:stimmbezirke` returns exactly 108 polygons — matches the city's stated 108 Wahlbezirke / Stimmbezirke for BTW 2025 and (per city press releases) BTW 2021. Sibling `kommunalwahlbezirke` (27 polygons) is the larger Kommunal-district layer, NOT WBZ. `stadtbezirke` (city-district) and `stadtteile` are also in the same workspace and would be the 9-Stadtteil / fewer-polygon objects to avoid confusing with WBZ.
- Schema is minimal: only `id` (3-digit Stimmbezirk number). No election year, no Wahllokal address, no eligible-voter count attached to the geometry. Joining to election results requires using the `Nr` field from the DKAN tables on geo.muelheim-ruhr.de.
- The DKAN open-data portal entries titled "Wahlbezirke" (resource ef3b30df-...) and "Stimmbezirke" (resource aa62afc5-...) are tabular results, NOT polygons — they were misleading on first inspection. Polygons are only via the WFS / Mapbender geoportal.
- 2013/2017/2021 historical snapshots: NOT publicly archived. Wayback returns no snapshots of the WFS. Best route is a direct request to Amt fuer Geodatenmanagement (geodaten@muelheim-ruhr.de) asking whether earlier election-cycle Stimmbezirk-Geometrien are retained internally; per city documents the count was already 108 in 2017 and 2021, so the current geometry is likely close but not identical (boundaries occasionally shift between cycles).
- Useful adjunct: each Stimmbezirk has a per-WBZ landing page on wahlpraesentation.muelheim-ruhr.de with results back to KW 2009; CSV-only (e.g. https://wahlpraesentation.muelheim-ruhr.de/bw25/05117000/daten/opendata/...). Not geometry but provides authoritative attribute-side keys.
- WMS for visual checks: https://geodienste.muelheim-ruhr.de/geoserver/gebietseinheiten/wms (same workspace).

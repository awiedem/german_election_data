## Notes

- Heilbronn is one of the better-covered Stadtkreise: three of four target years (2017, 2021, 2025) are published as proper Urnenwahlbezirk polygon layers on opendata.heilbronn.de, both as Esri Shapefile (EPSG:25832) and GeoJSON. The Wahlamt back-published the 2017 and 2021 layers on 2025-02-11 alongside the 2025 release.
- All three available layers were downloaded and verified: feature counts 86 / 66 / 76, single AGS 08121000, attribute `Bezirk-Art="W"` throughout (Wahlbezirk = polling-station district). Common attribute schema: `datum, ags, Bezirk-Nr, Bezirk-Name, Bezirk-Art, Wahlraum-Bezeichnung, Wahlraum-Adresse, Wahlraum-Barrierefrei, Wahlraum-Barrierefrei-Ergaenzung`. Briefwahlbezirke for 2017 are exposed as a separate non-spatial GeoJSON; 2021/2025 portal pages do not list a Briefwahl resource.
- BTW 2013 is the only gap. Tabular results live at `wahlen.heilbronn.de/btw13/` but no shapefile/geojson is on the open-data portal, FragDenStaat, govdata.de, or BW state portals (statistik-bw.de, daten-bw.de — these only carry Wahlkreis 267 polygon, not the ~86 WBZ inside Heilbronn). The Vermessungs- und Katasteramt is the right first contact (`vermessung@stadt-heilbronn.de`); secondary fallback is `wahlen@heilbronn.de`. An IFG/LIFG/UIG request via FragDenStaat is the formal path if the Amt does not respond.
- The interactive map at `karten.heilbronn.de/heilbronn/index.php?service=wahlbezirke` and the Geoportal at `gisserver.de/heilbronn/` render WBZ layers via WMS but do not expose the 2013 vintage as a downloadable layer (only current operational state).
- Same publisher pattern as Stuttgart but with the helpful difference that Heilbronn back-published prior elections; the gap structure is the inverse of Karlsruhe (which has 2013/2017 only as coarse aggregates and lacks 2021/2025 entirely).
- BW state geoportals (geoportal-bw.de, daten-bw.de) and govdata.de were checked — they aggregate the same opendata.heilbronn.de records via DCAT-AP harvesting; no additional vintages.

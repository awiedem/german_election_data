## Notes

- No city open-data portal exists (no opendata.mainz.de, no Mainz entry on GovData for Wahlbezirke geometries). Govdata search returned only other cities (Bielefeld, Muenster, Leipzig, Potsdam) for this dataset class.
- Mainz publishes WBZ assignments only as PDF Strassenverzeichnis (e.g. https://www.mainz.de/medien/internet/downloads/Strassenverzeichnis-Wahlbezirke-KW2024.pdf) and as an interactive map via the Wahlarchiv (https://wahl.mainz.de/wahlapp/, covers 2009-2025). The interactive map renders polygons but no download/WFS endpoint is exposed.
- Best institutional path: bauamt-geoinformation@stadt.mainz.de (holds the GIS layer) with cc to statistik@stadt.mainz.de (defines kleinraeumige Gliederung including Stimmbezirke) and wahlen@stadt.mainz.de (data owner for election-specific WBZ definitions). The Statistikstelle digitised the kleinraeumige Gliederung into GIS in the 1980s, so historical WBZ shapefiles for 2013/2017 likely exist internally.
- Note Stimmbezirke = Urne + Brief; for BTW 2025 Mainz reports ~369 Stimmbezirke total. Urnenwahlbezirke alone should be roughly half (~150-200 polygons), consistent with the requested scale.
- Mainz has 15 Stadtteile/Ortsbezirke - any layer of that size is NOT the WBZ layer.
- LVermGeo RLP (https://lvermgeo.rlp.de/geodaten-geoshop/open-data) does not redistribute municipal Wahlbezirke; only geobasis products.
